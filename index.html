Geographical Information System(GIS)

A geographical information system (GIS) is a system designed to capture, store, analyze, manage, and present spatial or geographic data. It integrates various types of data, such as maps, satellite imagery, aerial photographs, and tabular data, to provide a comprehensive view of geographic information.

Read more

Preparation of Physical Development Plans

Physical development plans, also known as physical development frameworks or master plans, are strategic documents that outline the long-term vision and goals for the physical development of a specific area or region. These plans provide a roadmap for guiding and managing growth, development, and land use within a defined geographic area.

Read more

Layout Plans

A layout plan, also known as a site plan or floor plan, is a detailed graphical representation of the arrangement and organization of various elements within a specific area. It provides a visual depiction of how different components, such as buildings, rooms, infrastructure, and open spaces, are positioned and related to each other.

Read more

Environmental Impact Assesment Report

Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) is a systematic process that evaluates the potential environmental, social, and economic effects of a proposed project or development. It aims to identify, predict, and mitigate the environmental impacts of a project before it is implemented.
